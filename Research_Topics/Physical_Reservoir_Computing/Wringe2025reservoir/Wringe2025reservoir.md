# Wringe2025reservoir — Reservoir Computing Benchmarks: A Tutorial Review and Critique

## 1. Core contribution

This review and critique examines the benchmark culture of reservoir computing. It argues that benchmark choice, variant definition, reporting practice, and baseline selection materially affect the claims researchers draw about reservoir quality. The contribution is a methodological audit of how RC tasks are used and misused. It is especially relevant to physical RC, where small implementation differences can already make comparisons difficult.

## 2. Method / mechanism / evidence

The authors organize common benchmark families, discuss memory-capacity measures, and identify ambiguities in tasks such as NARMA when versions are not specified cleanly. They emphasize reproducibility, comparator choice, and the need to separate task success from general reservoir characterization. The paper aggregates examples from the literature to show how loosely reported protocols can undermine interpretation. Its evidence is critical synthesis rather than new device experiments.

## 3. Key takeaway and limitation

The main takeaway is that RC benchmarking must be explicit enough to survive replication and fair comparison. A benchmark number without protocol detail can be less informative than it appears. The main limitation is that methodological critique alone cannot supply one perfect universal benchmark suite for every substrate. The paper raises the reporting standard, while the field still needs consensus infrastructure.

## 4. Open question

What minimal benchmark package and reporting template would let physical-reservoir papers remain comparable without flattening legitimate substrate-specific goals?
