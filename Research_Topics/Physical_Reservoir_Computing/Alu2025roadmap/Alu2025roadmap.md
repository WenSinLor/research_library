# Alu2025roadmap — Roadmap on Embodying Mechano-Intelligence and Computing in Functional Materials and Structures

## 1. Core contribution

This roadmap organizes a broad research agenda for mechano-intelligence: engineering systems in which materials and structures help sense, remember, compute, adapt, and act. Across twelve sections, it connects wave-based computing, neuromorphic and memory-bearing materials, soft matter logic, morphological computation, mechanical neural networks, physical reservoir computing, and robotics. The paper's central claim is not a single new device result, but that future adaptive systems should integrate intelligence more directly into physical substrates instead of relying only on centralized electronics. It frames this shift as relevant to autonomous robots, tunable structures, wearables, and other systems that need efficient, resilient interaction with their environments.

## 2. Method / mechanism / evidence

The paper supports its roadmap through a structured synthesis of recent work across materials, devices, and system-level demonstrations. Its physical-computing section highlights examples where nonlinear mechanical dynamics are used for information perception, decision-making, and locomotion, including physical reservoir computing in phononic metamaterials and origami robots. Other sections review mechanical memory, soft logic, morphological computation, and neuromorphic metamaterials as complementary building blocks for embodied intelligence. The evidence is therefore comparative and programmatic: the authors assemble concrete advances and use them to identify recurring design needs around memory, sensing, adaptive feedback, and integration with mechanical function.

## 3. Key takeaway and limitation

The main takeaway is that mechano-intelligence is emerging as a shared design direction across multiple communities, and physical computing is one plausible route for giving structures more local autonomy. The roadmap makes a strong case that intelligence-relevant functions should be considered jointly with materials, geometry, dynamics, sensing, and actuation rather than added afterward. Its main limitation is also inherent to the format: it surveys and argues across a very wide field instead of establishing a unified quantitative framework or directly comparing alternatives under a common benchmark. Several sections identify open foundations rather than mature design rules.

## 4. Open question

What systematic co-design framework can map a desired intelligent engineering function onto physical structure, material choice, sensing layout, and computing mechanism while also preserving ordinary mechanical requirements such as strength, dynamics, and robustness?
