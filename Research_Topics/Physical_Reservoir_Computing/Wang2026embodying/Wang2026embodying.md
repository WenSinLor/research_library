# Wang2026embodying — Embodying Physical Computing into Soft Robots

## 1. Core contribution

This perspective reviews how physical computing concepts can be embedded into soft robots. It covers analog oscillatory control, physical reservoir computing, and mechanical logic as complementary ways to reduce digital control burden or exploit body dynamics directly. The contribution is a systems-level argument that physical computing should be integrated with soft robotic design rather than treated as an isolated hardware curiosity. It explicitly frames digital and physical computing as potentially complementary.

## 2. Method / mechanism / evidence

The authors synthesize examples across rhythmic motion generation, perception, embodied control, and programmable logic in soft systems. They compare different forms of physical computing by the behaviors they enable and the kinds of interfaces they require. The paper also discusses how future soft robots may combine physical and conventional electronics rather than replacing one with the other. Its evidence is a curated cross-domain perspective.

## 3. Key takeaway and limitation

The main takeaway is that soft robots provide a natural host for physical computation because their bodies already contain rich, task-relevant dynamics. Embedding computation into that physical substrate may improve responsiveness, simplicity, or robustness in selected settings. The limitation is that the article is a perspective, so it does not benchmark one architecture against alternatives under a common protocol. It clarifies the design space more than it settles the engineering hierarchy.

## 4. Open question

What hybrid control architecture best divides labor between physical computation in the body and digital computation in the controller?
