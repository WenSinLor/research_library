# Khairnar2026spider — Spider Web-Inspired Sensing and Computation with Fiber Network Physical Reservoirs

## 1. Core contribution

This paper develops spider-web-inspired fiber reservoirs for sensing and computation. It shows that topology, spacing, pretension, and input conditions shape how much useful processing the network provides. The contribution is a more systematic design study of compliant fiber reservoirs that links morphology to benchmark capability and sensing efficiency. It pushes the earlier fiber-network idea toward tunable reservoir design.

## 2. Method / mechanism / evidence

The authors compare network families such as crosshatch and polygonal layouts, examine reservoir capacity under different mechanical settings, and test physical networks on NARMA-style tasks. They also study feature downselection, reporting that strong performance can persist after discarding a substantial fraction of candidate readout features. The paper finds crosshatch designs particularly competitive in the reported experiments. The evidence combines physical prototypes with quantitative task evaluation and architecture comparisons.

## 3. Key takeaway and limitation

The key takeaway is that compliant network morphology is not decorative; it directly changes the computation available to a physical reservoir. The feature-pruning results also suggest that sensing layouts may be reducible without destroying performance. The main limitation is that the best design choices still depend on the chosen tasks and measurement arrangement. The paper advances design heuristics, but not a universal web-like reservoir blueprint.

## 4. Open question

Can topology and sensor placement be co-designed automatically so that fiber reservoirs adapt to a target sensing task before fabrication?
