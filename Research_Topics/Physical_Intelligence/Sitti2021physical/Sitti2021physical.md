# Sitti2021physical — Physical Intelligence as a New Paradigm

## 1. Core contribution

This perspective defines physical intelligence as intelligence encoded directly into an agent's body through sensing, actuation, control, memory, logic, computation, adaptation, learning, and decision-making. It argues that autonomous machines should advance physical intelligence alongside computational intelligence, especially when operating in unstructured environments or at scales where onboard electronics are limited. The paper's main contribution is to establish PI as a unifying paradigm across mechanics, robotics, materials, biology, self-assembly, and collective systems. It also distinguishes PI from embodied intelligence by focusing specifically on intelligence realized in the body itself.

## 2. Method / mechanism / evidence

The paper builds its case through a conceptual synthesis of biological and engineered examples rather than a new experimental system. It discusses how PI dominates in some biological organisms, how mechanical feedback, pneumatic logic, mechanical memory, adaptive morphology, taxis, and collective physical interactions can create intelligent behavior, and how these mechanisms vary across length scales. The article uses examples ranging from mechanical governors and soft robots to slime molds, Venus flytraps, and swarms to show that sensing, decision-making, and adaptation need not be purely neural or digital. Its evidence is therefore comparative and programmatic: a structured argument that many dispersed fields already contribute pieces of a broader PI framework.

## 3. Key takeaway and limitation

The key takeaway is that future autonomous machines may become more capable by embedding useful intelligence into their bodies instead of assigning every sensing and control burden to centralized computation. This is especially relevant for soft robots, miniature machines, harsh environments, and systems where energy, latency, or hardware scale constrain conventional control. The main limitation is that the paper is a paradigm-setting perspective, not a quantitative design framework or benchmarked comparison of PI strategies. It identifies a research agenda more than it validates a single implementation path.

## 4. Open question

What engineering framework can determine which parts of an agent's sensing, memory, control, and decision-making should be physically embodied and which should remain computational?
