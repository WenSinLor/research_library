# Momeni2025training — Training of Physical Neural Networks

## 1. Core contribution

This review organizes the emerging problem of how to train physical neural networks at useful scale. It argues that the main challenge is not only building physical computing substrates, but also obtaining gradients or viable learning signals when the computation is embodied in hardware. The paper surveys software-side and hardware-aware training strategies rather than proposing a single new algorithm. Its contribution is a map of the training landscape for physical neural networks and the tradeoffs among available approaches.

## 2. Method / mechanism / evidence

The review covers in-silico training, physics-aware backpropagation, direct feedback alignment and local methods, zeroth-order schemes, physical gradient descent, adjoint approaches, equilibrium-propagation-style methods, and echo-backpropagation ideas. It compares these approaches by scalability, use of hardware gradients, demonstrated dataset size, and compatibility with different physical substrates. The extracted discussion repeatedly emphasizes mismatch between digital simulations and real physical devices, along with the possible energy advantage of training more directly in hardware. Evidence is synthesized across the literature rather than generated by one new experiment.

## 3. Key takeaway and limitation

The central takeaway is that training remains the bottleneck for physical neural networks, especially when one wants both hardware realism and scale. The review is useful because it separates gradient access, device mismatch, and system-level scalability instead of treating training as a single monolithic problem. Its limitation is that the field is moving quickly and many methods remain demonstrated only on modest systems or narrow device classes. No universally dominant training paradigm has emerged.

## 4. Open question

Which training strategy will first deliver a convincing combination of scalability, robustness to hardware mismatch, and net energy benefit over digital baselines?
