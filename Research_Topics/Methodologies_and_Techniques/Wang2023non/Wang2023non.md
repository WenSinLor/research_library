# Wang2023non-reciprocal — Non-Reciprocal and Non-Newtonian Mechanical Metamaterials

## 1. Core contribution

This paper designs elastic metamaterials that combine non-Newtonian-like velocity-dependent response with directional non-reciprocity. The authors aim to realize solids whose effective stiffness changes strongly with loading conditions and direction, yielding unidirectional shock resistance. The contribution is both a general effective mass-spring design picture and concrete 2D and 3D metamaterial realizations. It extends the language of non-Newtonian behavior from fluids into engineered solids.

## 2. Method / mechanism / evidence

The paper develops an effective model built from a main mass-spring system, resonant subsystems, direction-changing converters, and clipping boundaries. It then designs specific metamaterial cells with finite-element analysis and fabricates examples at centimetric and micrometric scales using distinct 3D-printing methods. Dynamic experiments verify velocity-dependent response and reveal non-reciprocal behavior when the impact direction is reversed. The discussion reports stiffness changes exceeding an order of magnitude with loading velocity in the studied structures.

## 3. Key takeaway and limitation

The key takeaway is that local resonance and directional mechanical conversion can create solids whose resistance is both rate dependent and direction dependent. This makes the framework interesting for impact management, docking, and adaptive protective structures. The main limitation is that the effects depend on carefully designed resonant subsystems and impact regimes, so broad-band generalization and practical durability are not fully settled. The work is a strong mechanical proof of concept rather than a finished application platform.

## 4. Open question

Can non-Newtonian mechanical metamaterials be designed to remain effective across wider impact spectra while preserving their directional selectivity?
