# Coulais2017static — Static Non-Reciprocity in Mechanical Metamaterials

## 1. Core contribution

This paper demonstrates that reciprocity can be broken in static mechanical systems, not only in wave-propagating dynamics. The authors design asymmetric nonlinear metamaterials whose output displacement depends strongly on which side is loaded. The central contribution is a route to one-way displacement amplification and mechanical isolation under quasi-static forcing. It extends non-reciprocity into a regime usually governed by the Maxwell-Betti reciprocity theorem.

## 2. Method / mechanism / evidence

The paper combines geometrical asymmetry with strong structural nonlinearity in fishbone-like metamaterials and related models. Experiments show visibly different displacement transmission when the same force is applied from opposite sides, including reported mechanical isolation around the instability regime. Reduced-order models and numerical simulations explain how asymmetry and nonlinear switching cooperate to produce large directional response. The work therefore supports its claim through a tight theory-model-experiment loop.

## 3. Key takeaway and limitation

The key takeaway is that static mechanical isolation becomes possible when reciprocity-breaking design combines asymmetry with nonlinear response. This widens the design space for soft robotics, protection, and mechanical signal routing. The main limitation is that strong effects rely on operating near nonlinear instabilities, which can narrow usable force ranges and complicate predictable deployment. The mechanism is powerful, but its robustness is inherently tied to carefully tuned structural behavior.

## 4. Open question

Can static non-reciprocity be engineered to remain large across broader load ranges without depending so strongly on instability thresholds?
