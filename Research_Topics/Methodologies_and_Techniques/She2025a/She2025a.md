# She2025a — A Generative Framework for Structural Crack Discovery under Limited Sensing

## 1. Core contribution

This paper proposes SparseGen-Damage, a generative AI framework for identifying internal structural cracks from sparse sensing data. Its central idea is to convert limited point measurements into reconstructed full-field strain information, then infer crack geometry from that richer representation. The contribution is a perceive-reconstruct-identify pipeline designed for structural health monitoring under sensor scarcity. It aims to reduce sensing burden while keeping crack localization and quantification accurate.

## 2. Method / mechanism / evidence

The method combines three modules: an LSTM for variable-length sparse inputs, a conditional GAN for high-resolution strain-field reconstruction, and an FPN-based identification stage for crack-tip localization. The paper validates the approach on aluminum alloy plates with internal straight cracks and reports crack length errors under `4%` plus orientation errors below `2 degrees`. It also analyzes robustness to sensor density, crack length, and generalization to cases outside the training distribution. The evidence therefore combines a structured machine-learning architecture with experimental validation and targeted sensitivity studies.

## 3. Key takeaway and limitation

The key takeaway is that sparse sensors can support much richer damage inference when they are embedded in a reconstruction pipeline rather than used only for direct regression. This is valuable for structures where dense sensor deployment is impractical. The main limitation is that the demonstrated crack family and structural setting are still controlled, and reconstruction quality remains tied to the learned training distribution. The framework is promising for SHM, but its transfer to more irregular damage, noisier settings, and field deployment remains open.

## 4. Open question

How well does SparseGen-Damage generalize to complex, evolving crack morphologies and real operational noise that differ substantially from the training cases?
